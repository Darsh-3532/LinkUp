Social Network Analysis Methodology

Our research employs a comprehensive social network analysis (SNA) framework to examine startup ecosystem dynamics, focusing on relationship patterns, influence structures, and resource flow mechanisms that drive entrepreneurial success.

Analytical Framework

Network Construction

Multi-layered network construction using startup-investor, mentor-mentee, and co-founder relationships derived from Crunchbase, AngelList, and LinkedIn data sources.

Centrality Measures

Degree, betweenness, closeness, and eigenvector centrality calculations to identify influential actors and structural positions within the ecosystem.

Community Detection

Louvain algorithm implementation for identifying cohesive subgroups and analyzing intra-community vs. inter-community connection patterns.

Temporal Analysis

Longitudinal network evolution tracking to understand how ecosystem structures change over time and impact startup success rates.

Literature Review & Academic Foundations

Our research builds upon decades of social network analysis scholarship, entrepreneurship theory, and innovation ecosystem studies from leading academic institutions worldwide.

Theoretical Foundations

Social Capital Theory

Building on Coleman (1988) and Putnam (1995), we examine how network-embedded resources facilitate entrepreneurial action and startup success.

Key Authors: Coleman, Putnam, Burt, Lin

Structural Holes Theory

Burt's (2005) structural holes framework applied to identify brokerage opportunities and information advantages in startup ecosystems.

Citations: 847 references in our analysis

Innovation Ecosystem Theory

Integrating Adner & Kapoor (2010) ecosystem perspectives with network analysis to understand collaborative innovation patterns.

Applications: 23 ecosystem case studies

Seminal Publications

Granovetter, M. (1973)

"The Strength of Weak Ties" - American Journal of Sociology

Foundational work on weak tie importance in information diffusion and opportunity discovery.

Uzzi, B. (1997)

"Social Structure and Competition in Interfirm Networks" - Administrative Science Quarterly

Network embeddedness effects on firm performance and competitive advantage.

Stuart, T. & Sorenson, O. (2007)

"Strategic Networks and Entrepreneurial Ventures" - Strategic Entrepreneurship Journal

Network strategies for venture creation and growth in technology sectors.

Identified Research Gaps
